Student & Faculty Representation Insights

This section examines the representation of SC, ST, and OBC students and faculty in premier institutions. While policies mandate reservation, actual representation varies across academic levels and sectors, with significant underrepresentation often observed in postgraduate programs and faculty positions.

Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs)

IIT Delhi data for UG, PG, and PhD levels (2024-25) shows near compliance at UG level but shortfalls at PG & PhD. OBC data for IIT Delhi was not specified in the source for these levels. (Data from Report 1, Sec 3.1.1)

PhD admissions remain a concern: At IIT Delhi (2020), 15/31 depts. admitted no SC PhDs, 19/31 no ST PhDs. Similar trends at IIT Bombay (2020): 16/26 depts. no SC PhDs, 5/26 no ST PhDs. (Report 1, Sec 3.1.1)

Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs)

Faculty representation in IIMs is critically low. Across 13 IIMs: SC 5%, ST 1%, OBC 9.6% vs. mandates of 15%, 7.5%, 27%. (Data from Report 2, Sec IV)

Unfilled Faculty Posts (7 IIMs)

OBC: 88, SC: 54, ST: 30 (Total Reserved: 172)

Unfilled Faculty Posts (11 IITs)

OBC: 415, SC: 234, ST: 129 (Total Reserved: 778)

IIM Ahmedabad PGP FABM (2024-26) shows a significant drop from applicants to admitted students for reserved categories. (Report 1, Sec 3.2.1)

National Law Universities (NLUs)

NLSIU Bangalore and NLU Delhi intake for 2025. Survey of Top 5 NLUs (2021-22) indicates OBC representation can be below mandate. (Report 2, Table 2; Report 1, Sec 3.3.1)

All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS)

AIIMS Delhi MBBS admissions (2017-18) largely align with central norms. (Report 2, Table 2)

AIIMS Delhi NEET Cutoffs (2021-24) remain very high across all categories, indicating admission of top-performing reserved category students. (Report 1, Sec 3.4.1)

Academic Performance Trends

Academic performance is a key indicator. While admitted reserved category students are often highly capable, systemic disparities in foundational education can lead to an "input gap" and subsequent challenges.

The "Input Gap": JEE Advanced Median Score Gap (2013-2023)

Significant disparity in JEE Advanced median scores compared to general category. OBC gap cited as -20% (likely pandemic fall). (Report 1, Sec 3.1.2; Report 2, Table 3)

AIIMS Delhi: Lowest Selected NEET Percentile (2018)

Admitted students from all categories show high performance. (Report 2, Table 3)

Top 5 NLUs: 12th Grade >90% Score Percentage (2021-22 Survey)

Disparities in pre-university academic achievement. (Report 2, Table 3)

The Dropout Crisis

Significant dropout rates among SC, ST, and OBC students in Central Universities, IITs, and IIMs highlight systemic issues. Data for NLUs is not centrally maintained. (Report 1, Sec 3.1.2, 3.2.2; Report 2, Sec VI)

Dropout Numbers (SC, ST, OBC) - Approx. 2018-2023

Total 13,626 SC/ST/OBC students dropped out from CUs, IITs, and IIMs over five years. Some reports suggest higher numbers. (Report 2, Table 4)

Note: Dropout data for National Law Universities (NLUs) is not centrally maintained by the government.
